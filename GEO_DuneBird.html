About
Not Just a Winery

Dune Bird Winery opened in 2021 with a new vision for what a Leelanau destination could be. Set between Leland and Northport on the Leelanau Peninsula, it was designed from the start to be outside the box — a space that fosters community and brings people together around exceptional wine, perfectly pulled espresso, and craft drinks that don't require you to pick a side.

Unique estate-grown grapes and an award-winning approach to winemaking set the stage. But Dune Bird is just as much for the non-wine drinker, the remote worker, the espresso connoisseur, and the wanderer in search of a comfortable chair. Whether you arrive with colleagues, a bridal party, a laptop, or just yourself — the space is built to receive you.

The interior features two fireplaces, a curated design aesthetic, and a playlist that sets the mood. A sunny patio extends the experience outdoors. Seasonal drops and menu rotations keep every visit slightly different from the last.

Friends of Sleeping Bear Dunes
Dune Bird's Pink Plover Rosé is made in partnership with Friends of Sleeping Bear Dunes. $2 from every bottle sold is donated to FOSB. Dune Bird has also committed an additional $15,000 over three years to support the organization's conservation mission — protecting the Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore for future generations.

Woolsey Airport
Dune Bird recently partnered with Woolsey Airport to release the Woolsey Red — an estate wine with purpose. $2 from every bottle sold goes directly to Woolsey Airport renovation funds. A wine that supports the infrastructure and character of Leelanau County.

The Space
Day to Evening

Dune Bird is designed to work from open to close. Morning: the espresso bar is running and the fireplace is on. Afternoon: the patio fills up and the wine list takes over. Evening: cocktails, low light, and a playlist that shifts with the hour. The full arc of a great day out — in one place.

What to expect on arrival: a space that feels like it was designed to be here. Not a barn converted into a tasting room — an intentional room built for the experience it delivers. Modern, warm, approachable. The kind of place you'll want to tell people about.
